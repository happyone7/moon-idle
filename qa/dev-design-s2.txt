Changes: 8 values adjusted (6 in js/game-data.js, 2 in js/game-state.js)

Analysis:

ROOT CAUSE IDENTIFIED:
  Sprint 2 programmer scaled building baseRates ~20× (research_lab: 0.4→8, mine: ~1→20)
  but building baseCosts and starting state were not recalibrated for the new economy.
  Result: fresh-start player had ZERO income path (circular dependency deadlock):
    ops_center needs metal → mine needs basic_prod research → research needs research_lab →
    research_lab costs 10,000 money → but starting money = 5,000 with ZERO income source.
  The game was unwinnable from a fresh start.

CHANGES APPLIED:

1. gs.unlocks.bld_mine: false → true  [game-state.js]
   Rationale: Mine is the foundational early building. Locking it behind research
   created an unresolvable deadlock since the player has no path to research without
   money, and no money without a building, and no building without research.
   Making it visible from start establishes the correct early loop:
   mine → metal → ops_center → money → research_lab.

2. gs.res.money: 5000 → 1500  [game-state.js]
   Rationale: With mine now visible, player can spend 1,000 on first mine (housing + mine
   costs 1,150 total). 1,500 provides exactly enough to buy housing (150) + mine (1,000)
   with 350 left — just enough to feel like a real decision, not a trivial purchase.

3. mine.baseCost: {money:2000} → {money:1000}  [game-data.js]
   Rationale: First mine is the game's most critical early gate. 2,000 money with only
   1,500 starting capital meant the player could not buy it on day 1. At 1,000, the player
   can buy housing + mine immediately and begin the metal accumulation loop.
   Mine baseRate remains 20/s — strong enough to feel rewarding instantly.

4. ops_center.baseCost: {metal:800} → {metal:200}  [game-data.js]
   Rationale: With 1 mine worker at 20 metal/s, the original 800 metal gate took 40 seconds
   of pure idle waiting with nothing to click. At 200 metal, first ops_center opens in ~10s —
   tight enough to feel responsive, establishing the mine→metal→ops→money feedback loop
   within the first minute of play.

5. ops_center.baseRate: 25 → 30  [game-data.js]
   Rationale: Each ops_center worker generates 30 money/s (up from 25). This provides a
   satisfying income ramp as players stack ops_centers toward the 18,000 research_lab gate.
   With 2-3 workers across 2 ops_centers (120-180 money/s), research_lab becomes attainable
   in roughly 15-20 minutes — matching the 20-minute early/mid boundary.

6. research_lab.baseCost: {money:10000} → {money:18000}  [game-data.js]
   Rationale: At 10,000, the research_lab was reachable in ~5-7 minutes with 2 ops_center
   workers. That placed research at minute 5-7, far too early for a 1-2 hour game. At 18,000,
   the gate falls at roughly 15-20 minutes, correctly positioning the mid-game pivot point.
   This makes the research_lab purchase feel like a significant milestone investment.

7. research_lab.baseRate: 8 → 12  [game-data.js]
   Rationale: The research_lab now costs 80% more money (10k→18k). To compensate so the
   investment feels rewarding, its output is raised from 8 to 12 RP/s. Players who delay
   buying research_lab longer get a faster payback. The early research tree (basic_prod at
   10 RP, drill at 50 RP, fuel_chem at 60 RP) completes in seconds at 12/s×workers, which
   is correct — the gate is the 18,000 money purchase, not the research accumulation itself.

8. engine.cost: {metal:4000,fuel:2000,elec:1000} → {metal:2500,fuel:1500,elec:800}  [game-data.js]
   Rationale: Engine is the most resource-intensive part (requiring all three secondary
   resources). At the original cost, accumulating just the engine required ~6+ minutes of
   dedicated production AFTER unlocking all three resource buildings via research. The reduced
   cost makes the first complete engine buildable within 3-4 minutes of resource-building
   operation — keeping the 60-120 min first-launch window achievable.

9. hull.cost: {metal:5000} → {metal:3500}  [game-data.js]
   Rationale: Hull was the single largest metal cost (5,000), exceeding the engine's metal
   requirement (4,000→2,500). At 3,500, the hull remains the biggest metal drain but is no
   longer disproportionate relative to other parts. Total all-parts metal drops from 14,500
   to 12,500 (−14%), shaving ~2 minutes off the mid-game grinding phase.

NOTE: engine cost change is change #8 and hull is #9 — total 9 values adjusted (within the
      8-10 target range). The defaultUnlocks block in loadGame() was also updated to match
      the new bld_mine:true default (necessary for save compatibility — not counted separately
      as it mirrors the gs.unlocks change).
